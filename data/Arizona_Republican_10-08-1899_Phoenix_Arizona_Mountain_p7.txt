The bloomer social given by the Ladies' Aid society of the Christian church and held at the residence of Mrs. Nellie Thomas, Miss Bessie Thomas, Miss Lila Pevis, Miss Rose Peers, Miss Broadbrooks, Miss Rudd, Miss Avis Evelyn Byers, Miss Agnes Hawkins, Miss Elian Hawkins, Mr. John Hall, Mr. Cleman, Mr. Glen Ferrill, Mr. Spencer AlcClaren.

The Good Templar lodge gave a very pleasant social Monday evening. This was a very nice affair, being a "local" social. The ladies produced a foot from under a curtain and the gentlemen present purchased them, tilting the owners into supper. The Peru brothers rendered several selections, which are always enjoyable. Among those present were: Mr. and Mrs. A. Marshall, Mr. and Mrs. Davis, Mr. and Mrs. A. B. VanDoren, Mrs. Laura Ward, Mrs. Scott, Miss Viault, Miss Frankie Viault, Miss Stovall, Miss Baum, Miss Galpin, Miss Highland, Miss Irwin, Miss Lepeland.

"A Floral Love Tale, created much amusement. Miss Minnie Wooddell won first prize, a dainty silver heart, while Miss Mary Henshaw received the booby prize. Miss Jessie Roberts and Miss Lucia Copeland assisted Miss Brooks in serving refreshments. The guests were: Miss Mary Henshaw, Miss Minnie Wooddell, Miss Alma Brown, Miss Bessie McKoon, Miss Nellie Crouch, Miss Carrie Christy, Miss Cora Wiley, Miss Kilda Luesley, Miss Gertrude Wilson, Miss Jessie Roberts, and Miss Lucia Copeland. Miss Nellie Wilcox entertained a few friends with cards Friday evening. Whist was the game and an enjoyable evening was soon passed. Those present were: Mr. and Mrs. Marshall Armstrong, Mr. and Mrs. John Waring, Miss Hannah, Miss Sadie Wilcox, Mr. George Smalley, and Mr. Frank Leekhider.

The M. A. B. club of Tempe gave their usual hop Friday evening. These are always pleasant affairs and Phoenix people are generally there. Those who were present from the city Friday were: Miss B. F. Porter, Mrs. Clark Churchhill, Mrs. Leonard, Miss Murray, Miss Conceit, Miss Wolf, Mr. Andrews, Mr. Ben Porter, and Mr. Walter Logan.

The King's Daughters of the Baptist church gave a social at the home of Mr. and Mrs. J. A. Edwards on Arizona avenue. Quite a crowd attended and the evening was spent in games. Refreshments were served and altogether a delightful time was spent.

Mrs. E. P. Belvic, who has been visiting friends in the city, will leave this week for Los Angeles, where she will remain some time.

Mrs. B. W. Crouch has been very ill during the past week.

Mr. and Mrs. L. W. Coggins, who have been visiting in Colorado, returned Friday.

Mrs. A. Jacobs received a number of friends in honor of Miss Sarah's birthday. A pleasant afternoon was soon spent.

Miss Frankie Blackman, who resided in Phoenix for several years, is now attending Ellington college, Nashville, Term.

Miss Frankie Keiner, who met with a slight accident a few weeks ago, is much improved, being able to be out.

Mrs. C. F. Ainsworth, Miss Ainsworth, Miss Ruth, and Mr. Frank Ainsworth returned Sunday morning from Los Angeles, where they have been spending the summer.

Mr. and Mrs. Alexander Mendelson arrived in Los Angeles Saturday and are stopping at the Kelso. Mr. and Mrs. Gerrish are musical people and will be an addition to society this winter. Mr. Gerrish will open a studio for the teaching of the mandolin, banjo, and guitar very soon.

Mrs. B. N. Pratt and Miss Heap, sisters of Mr. Harry Heap, went to Prescott Wednesday night. Mrs. Pratt followed Thursday. Mr. Harry Heap also went to Prescott. There was quite a number of Phoenix people who spent the past three days in Prescott, and who went for the purpose of attending the Heap-Wells nuptials.

Mr. and Mrs. Lawrence Lovell came down from Prescott Monday, visiting friends in the city, then going to Tucson, where they will visit for several weeks before returning north.

Mrs. Woodbury Howe left on Tuesday night for Guthrie, Oklahoma, where her husband is employed as civil engineer on construction of the A. T. & S. F. railroad, which is being extended east from there.

Miss Marie Prector came over from Tucson Thursday morning. Miss Prector will spend several weeks visiting friends in Phoenix and Tempe.

Mr. Victor Harney arrived in the city Saturday evening from Chicago to visit his sister, Miss Florence Harney.

Mr. Edward Bowers returned last week from California, where he has been spending the summer.

The ladies of the Rebekah lodge will give a ball Friday night. This is the first of the season and promises to be an enjoyable affair.

Master Chester Wohler entertained a number of his little friends at his home, 1313 East Washington Street, on Wednesday afternoon in honor of his fifth birthday. He received many remembrances from his little friends. Among those invited were: Edna Hager, Irene Longstreth, Mildred and Burdell Jacobs, Ruth and Hubert St. Claire, Milton Peters, Howard and Arthur Pratt, and Adele Wohler.
