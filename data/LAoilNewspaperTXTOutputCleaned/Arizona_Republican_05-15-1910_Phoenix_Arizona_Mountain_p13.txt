Mrs. Shirley Christy proved herself
a charming hostess Wednesday when
she entertained the members of the
Friday Club at her delightful country
home on the Christy road. The affair
was in the form of a picnic and a
delicious turkey dinner was served.
The place cards were attached to a
little verse about each member, which
were written by the hostess. Those
who enjoyed the day were Mrs. J. H.
McClintock, Mrs. Walter Bennett, Mrs.
J. H. Kibbey, Mrs. L. H. Chalmers,
Mrs. W. K. James, Mrs. Sims Ely,

Miss Elsie Munger will accompany
Miss Ruby Forderer to her home in
Alabama on Thursday, where she will
be Miss Forderer's guest until fall.
The Euchre club held three meetings
last week, the regular Monday meeting
closing the season's series of games.
Mrs. W. K. James and Mrs.
Leo Goldman made the highest scores
for the year. On Tuesday evening Mr.
and Mrs. James entertained the club
and the prize was won by Leo Goldman.
Again on Thursday evening the
club met with Mrs. Harry Jessop and
W. J. James made the high score.
On May 7 Miss Hazel Osborne,
daughter of Mr. and Mrs. J. W.
Osborne, was married to William Harris
at the home of the bride's sister, Mrs.
L. E. Foster, in Tucson. The wedding
came as quite a surprise to the many
friends of the bride. Miss Osborne
was raised in Phoenix and is an estim
able young lady. Mr. Harris is a well
known mining man of Bisbee, at which
place the young couple will make their
home. Mr. and Mrs. Harris spent seve
ral days of last week in town visiting
relatives.

Mr. and Mrs. Frederick Rabinnowitz
announce the engagement of their
daughter, Miss Amelia Rabinnowitz, to
Earl Curtis. Miss Rabinnowitz has
made a great many friends by her
sweet and charming manner during her
short residence in Phoenix. Mr. Curtis
is connected with the district court
and is one of the rising young Phoenix
men. The wedding will occur on the
13th of June at the home of the bride.
Mrs. Royal Lescher will leave the
last of next week for Buffalo, New
York. After a visit of several weeks
with her mother she will visit friends
in Atlantic City, also probably taking
a trip up the St. Lawrence before re
turning to Phoenix in October.
Mr. and Mrs. K. A. Kirk and Miss
Marjorie Grant left Saturday for New
York. After a visit of several weeks
in New York city, they will take an
ocean trip to New Orleans, returning
to Phoenix in about six weeks.
Mr. and Mrs. Harry Kennedy deli
ghtfully entertained the Neighborhood
bridge club last week. The high
scores were made by Mrs. E. T. Col
lings and Mr. Kennedy. The club will
meet this week with Dr. and Mrs. W.
G. Leitz at their home on North Cen
tral avenue.
Mrs. J. W. Dorris left last night for
Washington, D. C., where she will
join her daughter, Miss Rema Dorris,
and they will make a short visit in
Mississippi, returning to Phoenix in
July.
Mrs. J. K. B. Alexander will leave
the 4th of June for New York from
which place she will sail on the 13th
for Naples. Mrs. Alexander expects
to spend about three months touring
Europe.
Mr. and Mrs. W. G. Hartranft left
Monday for a week's visit in Los An
geles. Mr. and Mrs. Hartranft will
leave on the 27th for an extended visit
in the East, returning to Phoenix in
the fall.
