If you are interested in the colors Fossil is a deep grey; quartz is a steel grey; sultan yellow is a brilliant orange, and callan red is the gorgeous sunset note.
The purples range from daffodil,
which is a yellowish purple, to laven
der, parme, new purple which is deep
and rich. Castor is a leaf grey and the
new green is walnut shell green.
On many of the spring designs this
year there is much simple, but very ar
tistic decoration. Sometimes it is satin
in straps and at others fine braid,
sometimes a touch of exquisite passe
menterie and at others the strong con
trast of widely different materials. In
the cutting there are few radical de
partures from the lines in vogue last
year. The plaits fit a little closer,
and now and then an attempt is made
to show the handsomer lines of the
wearer's figure.
One of these attractive suits is made
of Vicuna cloth with medium finish
and of rather finer texture than usual.
The skirt falls smoothly in front,
forms two folds on each side and three
folds in the back. The waist is a mod
ified blouse, with but little pouching.
It has a novel rolling front and a high
stand-up collar. The sleeves are neat,
following the arm from the waist al
most to the shoulder, where they are
lightly puffed and stiffened on the up
per side. The lining is of taffeta silk
in Roman stripe or fancy mixture.
The collar, front, folds and sleeves of
the blouse and the front and sides of
the skirt are handsomely embroidered
in silk braid. The suit is bound to
wear well, vicuna cloth being almost
equal to broadcloth in its strength,
durability and other excellent qualities.
Vicuna cloth is one of the neatest and
comeliest of woolens, and its attract
iveness is but little changed by wear,
rain or rough usage.
The Easter services at the Phoenix
churches were thoroughly enjoyed.
The display of flowers was unusually
rich, and the music was of a very high
order. At the Presbyterian church
Miss Sargent sang "Easter Bells," with
violin accompaniment by Mr. Feuerstein,
in her most effective style. The
selections by the choir were exceedingly
happy. At the Episcopal church the
large congregation was delighted with
Messinger. Miss Cora Brill sang a solo
at vespers at the Catholic church.
The Philharmonic society will give
a concert for the benefit of the pro
posed library sometime in May.
The recital given by Miss Margaret
Randal at the M. E. church Tuesday
evening attracted a large and fashion
able audience. The program was a
varied and most attractive one. Miss
Randal is an artist in her profession
and her selections were well calcu
lated to show the wide range of her
talent. Miss Randal was assisted by
Mr. Andrews and Miss McKinney. Mr.
Andrews' rendering of the "Armorer's
Song" from Robin Hood was particu
larly pleasing. Miss McKinney sang a
sparkling ballad entitled "Almond
Blossoms," which was enthusiastically
received. The violin solo by Mr. Halsey
was especially good. Last on the
program, but by no means least, were
the "Scarf Fantastics" participated in
by Miss Plank, Miss Graham,
Miss Halsey, Miss Addine Bury,
Miss Sherritt, Miss Nutter, Miss
McKinney, Misses Tweed and Miss
Smith. The graceful postures and
charmingly simple costumes of the
young ladies were a source of artistic
satisfaction to the audience. The in
tricate tableaux, executed with admir
able ease and skill, were particularly
effective.

The afternoon tea for the benefit of
the library fund given yesterday at
the home of Mrs. Walter Talbot, was
not very well attended owing to the
inclemency of the weather. Miss Nannie
Kennedy, who has lately returned from
Denver, read two selections in a very
pleasing manner, and Mrs. Barnes sang.
Much amusement was afforded
by two ladies who told fortunes, and
Miss Kennedy won a prize for guessing
the correct number of beans in a bottle.
Delicious fruit punch and cake were served.

tickets from eastern points to Phoenix
and return. Phoenix to Los Angeles
and return, $30 each; Phoenix to San
Diego and return, $36.50 each; Phoe
nix to San Francisco and return, $50
each. This is done in order to give
eastern visitors an opportunity of see
ing the various points of interest on
the Pacific coast without great ex
pense. Tickets are on sale any date,
and are good for stop over at any and
all points, the final limit being gauged
by the limit of the round trip ticket
held by the passenger. For further

NOTICE TO VOTERS.
On April 2, 1898, a register of the
voters of the Second and Fourth ward
of the City of Phoenix, A. T., will be
opened at the city recorder's office in
the city hall of said city, for the entry
of the names of the electors entitled to
vote at an election to be held in said
Second and Fourth wards, May 3,
1898, for the election of a member of
the Common Council of the City of
Phoenix in each of said wards and
for voting upon such other matters as
may be by law then submitted to such
electors. Such registration will close
April 18, 1898.
Dated at Phoenix, March 31, 1898.
T. A. JOBS, City Recorder.
