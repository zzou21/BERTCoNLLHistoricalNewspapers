To prevent pastry from burning, set

vent their molding.

Sash curtains are preferable to all
others for the kitchen, as they are
easily removed and washed.

HILDRETH TO REOPEN
IN CRAIG BUILDING
Left Last Night to Purchase a New
Stock of Goods.

The famed
Adams Pharmacy, like the
phoenix bird, has risen from
the ashes. F. Hildreth, the
proprietor of the popular drugstore,
which evaporated in smoke along with
the Adams hotel, was exceedingly hard
hit by the fire; his insurance was rela
tively small. But like the typical
business man of Phoenix, he is 'game.'
He yesterday rented the corner of the
new Craig building on Center and
Monroe streets, immediately north of
the postoffice, and tonight he leaves
for Los Angeles with Mr. Cate, his
manager, to purchase a new stock.
Meanwhile the new store is open for
prescription trade, and within a few
days it will be ready to handle every
branch of the drug business. Mr. Hil
dreth had just purchased one of the
finest soda fountains ever seen in the
city, and fortunately it had not been
installed when the fire came. The new
fountain will be among the appoint
ments of the new store.

HE PREFERRED DEATH
TO LIFE
The Suicide of a Convict in the Yava
pai Jail.

But for a tragedy in the county jail
at Prescott yesterday morning Sheriff
Smith, who arrived in the city yesterday
afternoon would have had a companion
and business that would have taken
him to Florence. He also would have
had come to him a snug sum for mile
age which he has now missed.
The tragedy was the suicide of John
Bryant who had been convicted of
murder in the second degree and was
to be sentenced yesterday morning, the
first thing on the opening of court.
But when Deputy Sheriff George
Heisler went to the cell of Bryant he
found him dead. The old man had
hanged himself with a towel. One end
had been fastened to a bar and the
other about his neck. Then he had
lain down and choked to death.
Bryant had been a resident of
Yavapai county for thirty years and
had been engaged in mining and pros
pecting. On February 4 of this year
he killed S. H. Anderson at Silver
Butte. The men had some trouble
over a trifling financial matter and
Anderson was killed at his cabin with
a shotgun. Bryant claimed that he
had fired in self defense and after An
derson had shot at him with a revolver.
