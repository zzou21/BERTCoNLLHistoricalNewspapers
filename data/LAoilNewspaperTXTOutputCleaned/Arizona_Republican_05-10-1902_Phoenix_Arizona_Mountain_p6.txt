The new building of the Morenci Club
was formally opened on Monday of last
week with a programme, the main fea
ture of which was a lecture by Dr.
Waldo-mar Lindgren on "The Clearing
of History of the Morenci District."
This opening event had been looked
for and looked for anxiously by the people of
the camp for a long time, but the
highest expectations were fully
satisfied by the magnificent club building which
contains a large room and library, sup
plied with all the leading daily papers
and monthly magazines, as well as the
standard novels and scientific journals.
There are also about fifteen hundred
volumes of choice fiction, history, bi
ography, etc., in the circulating library in
connection with the club.
The main floor in addition to the
public reading room is located the gymnasium,
supplied with the most modern
apparatus for the purpose of exercise for
the body.
The baths fitted promptly. There is also on the same floor the
dance hall admirably adapted for its purpose.
Its attendants, the librarian's office occupies a central
position on the main floor and chairs
are placed in convenient sites to receive visitors.

This fine building is the finest
located for the bowling alleys, billiard and
pool tables, the bath rooms and the
pool room parlors, which are
fitted with the necessary apparatus for
various purposes.

Many complaints have been made
by local merchants over the dumping of
cheap goods into the market by outside
houses. The goods are often inferior in
quality and ruin local trade. The merchants
ask the public to be careful in buying.

The economic condition of the country
has been discussed by many speakers
during the past week. Several manufactories
are expanding and new concerns are
being established which promise increased employment.

DISTRICT ATTORNEY LING RESIGNS.

District Attorney Ling of Maricopa county
has resigned his position. The resignation
was telegraphed from Chicago. Ling
states that his business demands his
personal attention and he cannot longer
discharge his duties as district attorney.
The board accepted his resignation.
Ling has been an efficient officer and his
departure is regretted by many.

Dr. D. I. Cairns and ranchers
from the county seat attended the
meeting of the Board of Supervisors at
Florence today. Several matters of local
importance were discussed and acts taken
relative to road work.

The county fair will be held next fall and
local boosters are making plans to secure
a large attendance. New exhibits will be
added and the committee is at work to
secure attractions.

Lots of interest in the mining district
has been developing. Reports from the
upper camps indicate encouraging find
ings. The railroad companies have been
surveying new lines and location of
stations will follow.

The school trustees are preparing for
examinations and graduation exercises to
be held the last week in May. A number
of pupils will graduate this year and the
exercises promise to be interesting.

Mr. and Mrs. Hawes entertained last
evening at a musicale, the guests being
members of the Young People's Society.
The program included vocal and instrumen
tal numbers and was well rendered. Re
freshments were served.

A New Club Building.

The new club building of the Morenci
club is a fine addition to the town and
reflects much credit on the men who
planned and erected it. The interior is
well arranged and the reading room and
library will be of great benefit to the
community.

May Day Picnic.

The May Day picnic arranged by the
Sunday school will be held at the river
park next Wednesday. A number of
families have signified their intention to
go and the general committee promises a
pleasant occasion.

The music this season has been particu
larly good and the local orchestra has re
ceived much praise for its selections. Sev
eral new pieces have been added to the
repertoire.

The Phoenix Musical Club gave an en
tertaining concert last week and it was
well attended. The quality of the music
was high and the audience showed its ap
preciation by hearty applause.

There have been several visitors from
the outlying towns who came in to attend
the business meeting of the agricultural
society. The meeting closed with plans
for the fall exhibition.

The Arizona Republican has printed the
county fair program and copies may be
obtained at the office.

Funeral of Deceased Miner.

A funeral service was held yesterday
for John Matthews, who died at the hos
pital after an illness of several weeks.
He was a longtime resident and leaves
a family. The services were conducted
by Rev. Miller and the remains were
buried in the city cemetery.

The May Day festival given by the
primary class of the public school in the
park Friday was well attended. The
children participated in the exercises and
songs and the display of work was satis
factory. Refreshments were served.

Why Order of Sale of Real Estate
Should Not Be Made.

In the Probate Court of the County of
Maricopa, Territory of Arizona.
In the matter of the estate of Charles
Kinstler, deceased.
It appearing to this court by the pe
tition this day presented and filed by
Richard L. Rogers, the administrator
of the estate of Charles Kinstler, de
ceased, that it is necessary to sell the
whole or some portion of the real es
tate of said decedent to pay the debts
of decedent and the expenses and
charges of administration.
It is therefore ordered by this court
that all persons interested in the es
tate of said deceased appear before the
said probate court, on Wednesday, the
8th day of June, A. D., 1902, at the hour
of 10 o'clock a. m., of said day, at the
court room of said court, at the court
house in the city of Phoenix, Marico
pa county, territory of Arizona, to
show cause why an order should not be
granted to said Richard L. Rogers,
administrator, to sell so much of the
said real estate as shall be necessary
and that a copy of this order be pub
lished four successive weeks in the Arizona
Republican, a newspaper printed and
published in the said county of Mari
copa. Dated May 6th, 1902.
J. H. LANGSTON.
Judge of the Probate Court.
