A very pretty home wedding at
that of Miss Mary Hough and Edwin
Jones of Bisbee, which took place at
the home of the bride's parents, Mr.
and Mrs. Hough Thursday afternoon.
Reverend Campbell officiated using
the beautiful ring service. Quantities
of pink and white crepe decorations
and sweet peas were used with
charming effect in decorating the parlor
and hall. Promptly at 4:30 p. m.
C. R. Sterling sang "Because" after
which to the strains of Mendelssohn's
wedding march played by Mrs. A. G.
Hulett, the bridal party entered. Miss
Carrol Hough, sister of the bride, was
maid of honor and a dainty attendant
showed a bouquet of pink roses. Hal
bert Miller of Tempe was best man.
Then followed the bride on the arm of
her father. The bride's becoming cos
tume was of soft silk, with bridal veil
fastened with a crown of orange
blossoms. Immediately following the
ceremony a supper was served at
small tables, during which Mrs. Merrill of Clifton sang several enjoyable
solos. Mr. and Mrs. Jones left on the
evening train for Bisbee where they
will make their home in the future,
carrying with them the good wishes
of their many friends for a long life
of happiness.
