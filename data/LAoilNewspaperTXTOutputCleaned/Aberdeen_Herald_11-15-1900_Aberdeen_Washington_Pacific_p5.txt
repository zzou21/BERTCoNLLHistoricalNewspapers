Schooner Oliver J. Olsen Launched.

A large crowd gathered at Lindstrom
Bros.' shipyards last Thursday afternoon
to witness the launching of the schooner
Oliver J. Olsen, and quite a number
were on board the new vessel as she slid
down the ways.
Miss Annie Kauson, a teacher in the
Aberdeen Schools, christened the new
schooner by breaking a bottle of champagne,
contributed by W. J. Croebie for
the occasion, over the bow as the vessel
slipped gracefully into the water amid
the sound of the steam whistles in the
neighborhood.
The dimensions of the Olsen are:
length, 189 feet; beam, 33 feet 3 inches;
depth of hold, 14 feet; and the keel was
laid in time last. Her cost when completed,
will be, $44,000, and she will
have a carrying capacity of 900,000 feet
of lumber.
The new schooner will be commanded
by Capt. Oliver J. Olsen, for whom she
was turned, and who is one of the
youngest skippers on the Pacific Coast,
being just 34 years of age, and has
already been skipper of the barkentine
Ruth.

Local Accident.
Chas. Carder was struck on the
temple by a wire cable, at Green and
McHugh's logging camp, and instantly
killed yesterday afternoon.
The unfortunate man was about 55
years of age, and unmarried. He came
here from Castle Rock, Wash., last summer
and was a member of the Knights
of Pythias lodge at that place, in good
standing. Chancellor Commander, Geo.
J. Wiff of Wishkah lodge took charge
of the remains this morning and telegraphed
the father of the deceased at
Joplin, Missouri.
The remains will either be buried
here under the auspices of the Knights
of Pythias, or shipped to Missouri, as
the other may direct.
