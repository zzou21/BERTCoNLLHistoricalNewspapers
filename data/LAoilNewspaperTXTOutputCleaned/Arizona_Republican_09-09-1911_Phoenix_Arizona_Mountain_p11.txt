Having attended to this prelim
inary, Mr. Morton went to a printing
office and ordered some business

other stationery. All of these were
for the use of Mr. Morton as rep
resentative of the roofing company
aforesaid.

Finally, some time after dark

But yesterday when the check

Phoenix, Ariz., Sept. 5, 1911.

To the District Attorneys of Arizona:

Your telegram of September 6th re
ceived. You ask me to advise if per
sons registering subsequent to the
closing of the registration for the elec
tion of constitutional delegates, can
vote at the coming primaries to be
held October 24 and the state election
to be held December 12 next.

The question is of great importance
and has been bothering the district
attorneys throughout the territory, and
I have therefore given it close study.
and have requested and received the
advice of several of the leading at
torneys of the territory affiliated with
both the republican and democratic
parties. My opinion, and the reasons
therefor, will consequently be given
with some detail:

Section 19 of the Enabling Act pro-
vides that the qualified electors of
Arizona are authorized to vote for
delegates to a constitutional conven
tion; and that a qualified elector is
any male citizen of the United States
of the age of twenty-one years who
has resided in the territory at least
twelve months next preceding the date
fixed for the election for constitu
tional delegates, and who shall pos
sess in every respect the qualifications
of an elector as provided by Title 20
of the Revised Statutes of Arizona of
1901. Said section further provides
that the supervisors of each county
shall require a reregistration of the
qualified electors as above defined,
and a proviso is attached to the ef
fect that at a reregistration of the qual
ified electors whose names appear on
the great register for the year 1908 is
unnecessary but that such names to
gether with such as may register un
der the provisions of said section shall
constitute the great register of such
county and be used at each of the
elections provided for in the Enabling
Act.

Said section then provides further
that this provision shall apply to
voters at all elections for delegates to
the constitutional convention, for rat
ification of the constitution, for
state officers, members of the state
legislature, representatives in congress
and all other officers named in the
constitution or any others provided for
or mentioned in the Enabling Act.
From the provisions of said section
19, it would appear, therefore, that
qualified electors for all elections pro
vided for in the Enabling Act, would
be those registered under the re
registration aforesaid, that is to say,
those whose names appeared upon the
great register of each county for the
year 1908, and those additional per
sons who had registered thereafter
and up to twenty-one days prior to
the election of delegates to the con
stitutional convention.
It will be noted that the provision
of Chapter 3 of Title 20, Revised
Statutes of Arizona, is to govern in
the reregistration above referred to.
Paragraph 2303 of said chapter 3
title 20, provides that any one can
register twenty-one days before an
election and this explains my state
ment in the preceding paragraph that
only those registered in the great
register of 1908, and those registered
under the reregistration order of the
supervisors up to twenty-one days prior
to the holding of the election for del
egates to the constitutional convention.
would be entitled to vote.

Certainly it is clear from the pro
visions of section 19 of the Enabling
act, that only persons whose names
appear upon the great register of 1908
and those thereafter registering up to
twenty-one days prior to the election
for constitutional delegates, can vote
at any of the elections provided for in
the Enabling Act.

The congress of the United States
therefore, especially approved and rat
ified the provisions of "Election Ordi
nance Number 2" and its terms are
binding.
In reply to your telegram, therefore.
I have to state, that in my opinion, no
person registering since the closing of
the registration had prior to the elec
tion of constitutional delegates, can
vote at the coming primaries October
24th or at the coming election on De
cember 12th.
Respectfully submitted.
JOHN B. WRIGHT,
Attorney General of Arizona.

POTATO WAYS
Soak old potatoes in water.
Change the water while cooking if
strong.
Never allow potatoes to stand in
the sun.
Always pour off the water as soon
as done and remove the cover to
allow the steam to escape.
Potatoes cooked in the jackets
should be pierced to allow the steam
to escape.
Baked potatoes should be rolled in
a cloth till the skins burst. This
prevents the potatoes from cooking
too long, which makes them sticky
and soggy.
Potatoes should be simmered (not
boiled). It is a waste of fuel and
spoils the potatoes. Too rapid boil
ing makes them a solid paste which
is both unpalatable and indigestible.

Cook potatoes with the skins on as
often as possible. They are more
nourishing and palatable. Mashed
potatoes are delicious cooked in their
jackets peeled and mashed.
Potatoes may be kept warm a long
time without spoiling if the skins are
broken and the kettle well ventilated.
Potatoes well ventilated while
cooking are more wholesome and of
better flavor.
They may be baked, boiled, riced,
mashed, escalloped, potato balls,
potato salad, Saratoga
chips, shredded potatoes, creamed,
hashed brown, fried or saut√©ed, Lyon
naise potato and curried potatoes.
