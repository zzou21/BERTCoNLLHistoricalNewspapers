APPROVE MINERAL SURVEY
Surveyor General Ingalls yesterday
approved mineral survey No. 3233 for
the Side Hill, High Hill, Cora May,
Black Peak and Search Me lode min
ing claims, located in the Globe min
ing district of Gila County. The

WAIVES EXAMINATION Joe
Wilson waived examination in Judge
De Souza's court yesterday and was
bound over to the superior court.
Wilson was arrested on complaint of
Martin Gold, who claimed he took
mortgaged property out of this
county to Yuma county. The de
fendant's bond was placed at $500
which he failed to raise.
ENGAGEMENT ANNOUNCED
The engagement is announced of Miss
Ruth Hoover, of Boulder, and Wil
son Vinson, of Denver. The news is
especially interesting to Phoenicians
with whom Mr. Vinson is well
known. He is the son of the late
J. A. Vinson and Mrs. Vinson and
made his home here until a few
years ago, when he went to Denver
to enter the brokerage business. He
has been most successful. The wed
ding is scheduled for November 3.

EVERY GIRL IN PHOENIX Is in
vited to hear Mrs. Ray C. Harker
at the Y. W. C. A. this afternoon at
4 o'clock. Mrs. Harker is an enter
taining and forceful speaker. A few
weeks ago Mrs. Harker supplied the
pulpit of the First Methodist church,
those who heard her, speak in praise
of her ability. She will have a
worth while message. The afternoon
vespers for the fall season have just
begun. Many speak of the helpful
ness and cordiality of the hour. Spe
cial music will add to the interest of
the afternoon. A cordial invitation is
extended.
CELEBRATE COFFEE WEEK
To celebrate the American coffee
week, October 18 to 24, Miss Anna
Tipton, representing Ben Hur Cof
fee people, has established head
quarters for the week at the Bon
Ton Grocery, where she will dem
onstrate, serving free coffee and
wafers. She will be assisted in the
work by Miss Beatrice Millsap, a
young woman whose home is in
Phoenix. Ben Hur Coffee is put out
by Joannes Bros, of Los Angeles, and
long has been familiar to Arizona
households.
SCHROEDER-PONDER Charles C.
Schroeder, formerly employed in the
mechanical department of The Republi
can, but now with the McNeil company,
and Miss Henri Ponder, the charming
daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Benjamin H.
Ponder, were quietly married last eve
ning at the Central Methodist parson
age, Rev. W. J. Sims officiating. They
will continue to make their home in
this city. The bride was formerly em
ployed in the circulation department of
The Republican and afterwards as
cashier and bookkeeper for the Hackett
Market. A host of friends will extend
congratulations and good wishes.
MAKE GOOD CATCH Three
hours after the theft of a trunk con
taining $547 in cash and valuable
articles of clothing had been reported
to the police by the Mexican Circus
company, early yesterday morning,
the officers had landed Ramon Ru
elas and Francisco Sains in the city
jail and had recovered every cent of
the money and all of the stolen
goods. The young men confessed,
were arraigned in Magistrate Mc
Bride's court in the afternoon and
held to answer to the superior court.
Ramon Magus, who had been arrest
ed with the other two, was released,
it being shown he had no connection
with the robbery. The looted trunk
was found near the Arizona Eastern
round house and the men were found
at 540 West Lincoln. They had al
ready made a division of their spoils.

FARM IMPROVEMENT ASSN. OR
GANIZED A meeting was held in the
Scottsdale school house in charge of
Farm Advisor Armstrong yesterday
evening, at which time the Scottsdale
Farm Improvement Association was
organized. Following are the officers:
President, W. P. Smith; vice presi
dents, W. E. Kinsey and Charles Mil
ler; secretary-treasurer, Mrs. Stout.
Vice President Thomas R. Marshall
was present at the meeting and deliv
ered an interesting address. Mr. Arm
strong reports that a similar meeting
held at the Alhambra school house
last Thursday night and an improve
ment association was also formed for
that locality. At the meeting held at
the Pendergast school house last Wed
nesday evening a society was also
organized and C. L. Smith was elected
president; S. W. Wilkinson, vice presi
dent, and Paul Dreenhay, secretary
treasurer. A representative of the state
fair organization was present at all of
these meetings and gave the farmers
some interesting pointers regarding the
coming big show.

CHILDREN'S HOME OFFICER
HERE Mrs. J. F. Ebert, of Tucson,
state superintendent of the Arizona
Children's Home association, is a
guest at the home of Mrs. W. W.
Edwards on North Second street.
Mrs. Ebert expects to organize a
branch of this work in Phoenix, it be
ing a work that every man and wom
an should be interested in. All those
who have been approached regarding
this matter seem to think that it is
just what has been needed here for
years. Mrs. Ebert will leave for Ray
on Monday and will return about the
middle of the week to perfect the
work of organization in this city, a
notice of which will be published in
a subsequent issue.
